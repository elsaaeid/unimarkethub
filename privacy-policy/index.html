Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Unimarkethub (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, shares, and protects personal information in connection with our website and digital-marketing services. This template is for general information only and does not constitute legal advice.

1) Who we are

We Unimarkethub are a digital marketing agency.
Contact: info@unimarkethub.com

2) Information we collect

  • Contact & lead data: name, email, phone, company, role, message content (e.g., via forms, WhatsApp, live chat, or lead ads).
  • Marketing preferences: newsletter subscription, opt-in/out choices.
  • Usage data: pages viewed, links clicked, device/browser, IP address, approximate location, session events.
  • Cookies & similar tech: identifiers used for analytics, conversions, and ad personalisation/measurement.
  • Business communications: emails, call notes, meeting notes relevant to your requests.

We collect information directly from you, automatically via the site, and from partners (e.g., Meta Lead Ads, Google Lead Forms) where you grant permission.

3) How we use information

  • Provide and improve our site and services.
  • Respond to inquiries, provide proposals, and fulfill contracts.
  • Measure campaign performance and conversions.
  • Personalise content/ads and build lookalike audiences (where permitted).
  • Security, fraud prevention, and diagnostics.
  • Legal compliance and enforcing our agreements.

Legal bases (GDPR/UK GDPR): consent, contract performance, legitimate interests (e.g., B2B marketing and analytics balanced against your rights), and legal obligations.

4) Sharing & processors

We share data with vendors who help us run our business, under contracts that protect your information:

  • Analytics & measurement: Google Analytics/Tag Manager, Google Ads, Meta Pixel.
  • Advertising partners: Meta, Google, LinkedIn, and other ad networks.
  • CRM & email: [[HubSpot/Mailchimp/SES/etc.]].
  • Hosting & infrastructure: [[A2 Hosting/AWS/etc.]].
  • Payment/invoicing (if used): [[Stripe/PayPal/etc.]].

We disclose information if required by law, to protect rights and safety, or during business transfers (e.g., merger, acquisition).

5) Cookies & tracking

We use essential cookies (for site functionality) and non-essential cookies for analytics, advertising, and conversions.

Cookie categories
  • Essential: strictly necessary for the website and cannot be switched off.
  • Analytics: help us understand usage and improve the site.
  • Advertising: measure ads, personalise content, prevent fraud.
  • Functional: remember choices and enhance features.

6) Advertising & analytics

We may use Google Ads, Meta ads (Facebook/Instagram), LinkedIn Ads, and their pixels/tags to measure conversions and reach audiences. You can control interest-based ads through your platform settings and device ad settings.

7) Your privacy rights

  • Access & portability – request a copy of your data.
  • Rectification – correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure – ask us to delete where applicable.
  • Restriction & objection – including direct marketing opt-out.
  • Withdraw consent – where processing relies on consent.
  • California (CPRA) – right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of sale/sharing or targeted advertising.

Submit a request: info@unimarkethub.com. We may verify your identity before fulfilling requests. You may authorise an agent where allowed by law.

8) Security

We employ administrative, technical, and physical measures to protect personal data. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

9) Data retention

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described, to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Typical periods:

  • Lead/contact data: 12–36 months from last interaction, unless you request deletion earlier.
  • Analytics/ads identifiers: per vendor defaults or until you opt out.
  • Contracts/Invoices: per statutory requirements.

10) International transfers

Your data may be processed in countries different from your own. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses) for cross-border transfers.

11) Children’s privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13 (or applicable age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect such data. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us to delete it.

12) Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last Updated” date and, where appropriate, provide additional notice.
